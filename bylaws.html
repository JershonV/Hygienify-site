Compliance, Bylaws & Public Accountability

Corporate Governance

Hygienify Organization • IRS 501(c)(3) Transparency Hub

Part I — Constitutional Bylaws

Article I

Name & Purpose

Section 1. Name

The name of the organization is Hygienify.

Section 2. Purpose

Hygienify is organized exclusively for charitable purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

“Hygienify provides hygiene kits and essential supplies to underserved individuals in the St. Louis area to improve health, dignity, and well‑being through charitable distribution and community outreach.”

To accomplish this mission, the organization may conduct charitable programs, community outreach, volunteer initiatives, and any other activities consistent with Section 501(c)(3).

Section 3. Prohibited Activities

  • No part of the net earnings shall inure to the benefit of any private individual.
  • The organization shall not participate in political campaigns or substantial lobbying.
Article II

Membership

Hygienify shall have no voting members.

All authority rests with the Board of Directors.

Article III

Board of Directors

Section 1. Authority

The Board of Directors governs the organization, sets policy, and oversees finances and programs.

Section 2. Number of Directors

The Board shall consist of a minimum of three (3) directors.

Section 3. Qualifications

Directors must support the mission of the organization. Minors may serve as directors.

Section 4. Terms

Directors serve six‑year (6‑year) terms and may be re‑elected without term limits.

Section 5. Meetings

The Board shall meet at least once per year. Meetings may be held in person or electronically.

Section 6. Quorum

A majority of directors constitutes a quorum.

Section 7. Voting

Decisions are made by majority vote of directors present.

Section 8. Vacancies

Vacancies may be filled by majority vote of the remaining directors.

Section 9. Removal

A director may be removed by majority vote for failure to perform duties or conduct harmful to the organization.

Article IV

Officers

Section 1. Officers

The officers shall include a President, Treasurer, and Secretary. Additional officers may be created by the Board.

Section 2. Duties

  • President: Provides leadership, presides over meetings, and represents the organization.
  • Treasurer: Manages finances, prepares reports, and oversees compliance.
  • Secretary: Maintains records, minutes, and official documents.

Section 3. Terms

Officers serve six‑year terms and may be re‑elected.

Section 4. Minor Officers

Minors may serve as officers.

Article V

Conflict of Interest

  • Directors and officers must disclose any potential conflicts.
  • No director may vote on matters where they have a financial interest.
  • Family members may serve on the board, provided decisions are made in the organization’s best interest.
Article VI

Finances

Section 1. Fiscal Year

The fiscal year ends on December 31.

Section 2. Use of Funds

Funds must be used exclusively for charitable purposes.

Section 3. No Private Benefit

No part of the organization’s income may benefit private individuals except for reasonable compensation for services rendered.

Article VII

Amendments

These bylaws may be amended by majority vote of the Board of Directors.

Article VIII

Dissolution

Upon dissolution, assets shall be distributed for one or more exempt purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) or to a government entity for public purposes.

Part II — IRS Compliance Policies

IRS Form 1023 Mandate

Whistleblower & Record Retention Policy

Whistleblower Protection: Hygienify requires all directors, officers, and volunteers to observe high standards of business and personal ethics in the conduct of their duties. Anyone filing a complaint concerning a violation or suspected violation of financial or operational code must be protected from retaliation or adverse programmatic consequence.

Document Retention Rules: All operational corporate documentation, accounting logbooks, checks, bank records, board resolution minutes, and legal tax materials are maintained indefinitely. Standard general operational files are safely purged on a rolling 7-year regulatory compliance schedule.

Civil Rights & Inclusion

Equal Opportunity & Nondiscrimination

Hygienify is committed to fostering an inclusive, safe, and welcoming environment for all members of our community, including our volunteers, staff, partners, and the individuals we serve.

“Hygienify does not and shall not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, creed, gender, gender expression, age, national origin, disability, marital status, sexual orientation, or military status in any of its activities, operations, or distribution of goods.”

This policy applies to all aspects of our programs, including the distribution of hygiene kits and essential supplies, the selection of volunteers and board members, and all operational decisions.

Federal Transparency Provision

Public Disclosure & Tax Exemption Statements

Pursuant to Internal Revenue Code Section 6104, Hygienify maintains a strict transparency framework. Upon programmatic approval of our federal tax status, copies of our original IRS Form 1023 Application, corporate determination letters, and all consecutive annual IRS Form 990 information returns will be made immediately accessible here for physical public inspection and download.
